nvidia at five trillion

a company now worth more than the entire gdp of japan, the uk, and india
4 min read from @raysterminal

at some point in the last quarter, nvidia became worth more than japan. not more than toyota, or sony, or the entire japanese stock market. more than japan. the country. one hundred and twenty-five million people, the third largest economy on earth, producer of cars, robots, electronics, and anime. a chip company in santa clara passed it in market capitalization while everyone was watching something else.

the comparison is technically nonsensical. market cap is the total value of a company's outstanding shares. gdp is the total value of goods and services produced by a country in a year. they are not the same thing. you cannot buy japan. but the comparison persists because it is the only way human brains can process a number this large. five trillion is not a quantity, it is a scale error. you need to anchor it to something familiar, and countries are the largest familiar economic objects we have.

germany (gdp)$5.05T
japan (gdp)$4.44T
united kingdom (gdp)$3.89T
india (gdp)$3.94T
gdp figures from world bank / imf 2025 estimates. nvidia market cap from nasdaq, may 2026.

nvidia sits between germany and the concept of japan. it is worth more than the uk and india combined. if you had tried to explain this to an economist twenty years ago, they would have assumed you were describing a currency collapse or a data entry error. today it is just a Tuesday.

the company makes graphics processing units. originally for video games. then for cryptocurrency mining, briefly. now for training artificial intelligence models. the gpus are the picks and shovels of the ai gold rush, and nvidia owns the only shovel factory. every major tech company, every startup, every research lab, every government with an ai strategy is buying their chips by the ten-thousand-unit lot. the demand is so far ahead of supply that the lead time for their most advanced data center chips is measured in quarters, not weeks.

the valuation assumes this continues forever. that ai demand keeps scaling exponentially. that competitors like amd, intel, and the various custom silicon projects at google, amazon, and microsoft never catch up. that the ai boom does not burst, plateau, or get regulated into smaller margins. that is a lot of assumptions for a company that fundamentally makes very expensive calculators.

but the more interesting question is not whether nvidia is overvalued. it is what it means that a single company can exceed the economic output of a nation. japan has universal healthcare, a bullet train network, forty-seven prefectures, and a culture that has produced some of the most precise manufacturing on earth. nvidia has eleven thousand employees and a campus off the 101 freeway. the comparison is absurd because the things being compared are not comparable. but the absurdity is the point. the number is so large that normal comparisons fail.

historically, the largest companies in the world were worth fractions of a percent of global gdp. standard oil at its peak was perhaps one percent. microsoft at the height of the dot-com boom was a fraction of a percent. nvidia is approaching one percent of global gdp by itself. this has never happened with a company whose product is a component, not a finished good. nvidia does not sell cars or phones or software subscriptions. it sells the silicon that makes other people's products possible. it is a picks-and-shovels company that is worth more than most of the gold mines combined.

the risk is concentration. if ai demand slows, if chinese export controls tighten further, if a competitor releases a chip that matches performance at lower cost, if the major cloud providers succeed in building their own silicon at scale, nvidia's valuation has a long way to fall. five trillion is not a floor. it is a bet that the future will look exactly like the present, only more so. that is usually a bad bet. but it has been a profitable one for the last three years.
